# Email Composition

## Overview
Draft professional emails. Calibrate tone to audience and context. Be concise without being abrupt.

## Structure
1. **Subject**: Specific, actionable, 6-8 words
2. **Opening**: Context (why you're writing), 1-2 sentences
3. **Body**: Key information, bullet points for multiple items
4. **Call to action**: What you need from them, with deadline
5. **Closing**: Standard (not "Thanks!" for serious topics)

## Tone Calibration
- **Cold outreach**: Respectful, brief, clear value proposition
- **Status update**: Factual, forward-looking, flag blockers early
- **Disagreement**: Private, specific, solution-oriented
- **Apology**: Acknowledge, explain (not excuse), fix

## Rules
- One email = one topic. If there are two topics, send two emails.
- Put the ask in the first two sentences
- Never send an angry email — draft, wait, re-read, then decide

## Anti-Patterns
- Don't use "following up" as a subject line
- Don't CC people who don't need to act
- Don't write a novel — if it's >5 paragraphs, it's a meeting
